Tomaso Pignocchi

Philosophy · Paris · Kyoto

I am a Ph.D. candidate in philosophy at the Catholic University of Paris and the University of Rome–Lumsa, where I also teach moral philosophy. Currently I am a Guest Research Associate at Kyoto University. My research explores the dialogue between Wittgenstein's later philosophy and Buddhist thought — particularly Madhyamaka and the Indian epistemological tradition — with attention to questions of language, disagreement, and what it might mean for philosophy to function as a practice of transformation rather than a construction of doctrines. I also photograph, mostly in Rome and Kyoto.
